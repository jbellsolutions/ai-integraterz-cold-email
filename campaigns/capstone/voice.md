# Voice (universal AI Integraterz)

These rules apply to every cold email regardless of offer or angle. The agent self-validates against them before send. Same voice doc applies to `direct-value/voice.md`.

## Who is writing

Justin Bellware. Microsoft-certified AI CO / AI CIO / AI CTO. 10,000+ hours in AI engineering. 10+ years in technical recruiting before AI Integraterz. Burned ~$60K on generic VAs before he built the placement model. Built and ran a 5-person Philippines software team. Helped turn an Australian business from near-bankruptcy to 1,100% YoY growth using AI-native operations.

## Personality the email must carry

- **Reflective, not aggressive.** He thinks out loud. He does not pitch.
- **Specific over vague.** Specific numbers, specific industry nouns, specific dollars. Vague is worse than nothing.
- **Self-deprecating about the $60K.** Owns mistakes. Does not posture.
- **Generous with what he knows.** Will hand a prospect a free blueprint with no expectation of return.
- **Calm authority.** Never urgent, never anxious, never artificially scarce.

## We DO

- Plain language. Sixth-grade reading level should follow the entire email.
- **One specific number per email** — AICO hourly rate, sprint length, dollars, hours, percent.
- **One claim, one ask.** Not three of either.
- **Industry-specific nouns** wherever possible: intake, prior-auth, recall, dispatch, F&I, demand letter, outbound, content cadence. Generic words feel generic.
- Reference Microsoft-certified oversight **at most once per sequence** — embedded as a credibility cue, not bolted on. **Never in the first email.**
- Sign as **Justin** or **Justin Bellware**. Audit call is run by Justin personally — that promise must be visible in the signature.
- End with a clear ask in the form of a **question**. The reader should know exactly what saying yes means.

## We do NOT

- **No exclamation points.** Ever.
- **No emojis.** Ever.
- **No hype words:** revolutionary, game-changer, cutting-edge, transformative, leverage (as a verb), unleash, supercharge, 10x.
- **No fake urgency:** "limited spots", "only N seats", "this week only" — unless literally true.
- **No flattery openers:** "I came across your company and was impressed". Skip the compliment, lead with the relevance.
- **No "I hope this finds you well."** Cut it.
- **No three-paragraph value props.** If it is over 130 words, it is too long.
- **No selling on first email.** First email earns the right to send the second.
- **No buzzword references to AI as a thing.** Reference what AI does for them in their words.

## Slop ban list (hard reject if any present)

These get the AI-slop critic rejecting the draft outright:

- "I hope this finds you well" / "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I noticed your great work" / any LinkedIn-flavored flattery
- "Circle back" / "touch base" / "deep dive" / "let's connect" / "let's hop on"
- "Synergize" / "synergy" / "leverage" (as verb) / "leveraging"
- "Just following up" / "wanted to reach out" / "touching base"
- "Game-changing" / "revolutionary" / "cutting-edge"
- "Unlock the potential" / "in today's fast-paced"
- "I'd love to learn more about" / "I came across your profile"
- Em-dashes used as ChatGPT-style rhythm (≤1 per email; sign-off `-- Justin` is fine)

## Subject line constraints

- Lowercase
- Three to six words
- No punctuation other than commas
- Industry-specific noun if at all possible
- Target ≤33 chars (so it doesn't truncate on mobile)

## Sign-off

`-- Justin` or `-- Justin Bellware`. No corporate footer in body — auto-attached separately. The audit call run by Justin personally is part of the credibility — make sure that's never lost.
