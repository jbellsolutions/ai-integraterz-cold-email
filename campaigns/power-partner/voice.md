# Power Partner Voice Guide

## Voice attributes (Copy squad must hit)

| Attribute | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| Direct | Point in the first sentence. No throat-clearing. |
| Specific | Numbers, not adjectives. "$1,000 + $150/mo" not "great revenue". |
| Peer-to-peer | One operator to another. Not a vendor pitching a customer. |
| Proof-forward | Every claim backed by mechanism or number. |
| Human | Self-aware, occasionally wry. Acknowledge it's a cold email when appropriate. |
| Generous | Lead with what the partner gets, not what we want. |
| Qualifying | Selective, not desperate. "We're building this with 20 agencies." |

## Words to use

partner, deploy, introduce, revenue stream, revenue share, AI integrator, trained human + AI agents, your clients, your business, we handle everything, experience first, build together, selective, recurring, compounding, monthly income, introduction, your client relationships, plugged in.

## Words to avoid (HARD BAN)

affiliate, referral program, commission (use "revenue share"), sign up, join, opportunity, exclusive (unless genuinely limited), game-changing, revolutionary, cutting-edge, synergy, leverage (as verb), circle back, touch base, passive income, side hustle, disrupt, scale (without specifics), excited, incredible, unlock, just following up.

Plus the universal AI-slop ban (Copy Squad's `slop_critic`):
- em-dashes (use commas or rewrite)
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I noticed your great work" / LinkedIn flattery
- "deep dive", "circle back", "let's connect"
- Three-em-dash rhythm patterns ChatGPT loves

## Permission to

- Acknowledge the email is cold ("I'll be upfront — this is a cold email")
- Use humor as a pattern interrupt (not as a gimmick)
- Be direct about money in the first paragraph
- Walk away in Email 3 with confidence
- Use plain 12-year-old-readable language
- Assume the partnership makes sense — only the timing is in question
- Include one qualifying filter in Email 1 to filter the bottom 80%

## "NOT for you if" filter

The email must not sound like:
- An MLM recruitment pitch
- A corporate partnership deck
- A sales email wearing a partnership costume
- A growth hacker's "let me automate your pipeline"
- Anything that could appear on corporate LinkedIn without anyone blinking

## Top objection handlers

| Objection | Response (1 line) |
|---|---|
| "What stops you from going to my clients directly?" | Partner Protection Clause: non-solicitation, commission continuity, no upsell-poaching. Written into the partner agreement. |
| "How do I know it works?" | We deploy in your business first, free. You experience it before anyone is asked to do anything. |
| "I don't want to risk my client relationship" | 30-day double-output guarantee. They get results or they pay nothing. Your reputation is protected by our SLA. |
| "I've seen this before" | Most partner programs pay once. We pay every month for as long as the client stays. The income stacks. |
| "I'm too busy" | Your job is six words: "Let me introduce you to someone." We handle everything else. |
