# Direct Value (AICO / Expert Series) — Home Services niche overlay

## Why this niche

Home-services operators (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical, landscaping,
pest, restoration) live and die on speed-to-lead and dispatch efficiency.
AICO drops directly into the gap between phone-rings-but-nobody-answers and
revenue. The math is immediate: missed calls × avg ticket = visible $/month.

## Framing for AICO direct

The pitch is operational, not aspirational:
- **Speed-to-lead agent** — answers/qualifies/books inbound in 60 seconds
- **Dispatch agent** — routes jobs by tech location + skill in real time
- **Review-recovery agent** — re-engages 1-star calls before they post

Quantify in their language: "$X per missed call × Y missed calls/week."

## Expert Series angle

For owner-operators who run training programs, masterminds, or mentor other
shops (Tommy Mello-shaped, Service Titan partner ecosystem). The Expert Series
turns their operational playbook into an agent they sell to peer shops.

## Hook angles

- **Variant A (AICO)** — "Your phones miss ~22% of after-hours calls. I'll
  install an agent that answers + books them in under 60 seconds. 14 days."
- **Variant B (AICO)** — Lead with one metric: "What's your missed-call $
  number?" — then show the AICO that recovers it.
- **Variant C (Expert Series)** — "You've trained other shops on dispatch.
  Want to license it as an agent?"

## Red lines

- No "digital transformation" language. They want phones answered, not vision.
- Avoid talking about ChatGPT or LLMs. Talk about jobs booked.
- Don't insult their existing CRM/dispatch — meet them where they are.

## Soft CTA

"Want a 10-min Loom of the speed-to-lead agent built for a [HVAC/plumbing]
shop your size?" — concrete, fast, no commitment.
